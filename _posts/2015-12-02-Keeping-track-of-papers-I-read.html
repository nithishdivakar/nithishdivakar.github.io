KEEPING TRACK OF PAPERS I READ
WITH LATEX
Nithish Divakar
nithish.divakar@gmail.com
ABSTRACT
A good enough solution for keeping the bibliography entries of all the papers I
read with a small explanation of what is in the paper.
1 THE PROBLEM
My job involves me reading lots of research papers and later citing them in the works I do and
reports that I write. Over the time this has lead to some problems as follows.
1. Individual bib les for each report. Lots of bib les.
2. Not able to pinpoint the exact paper that I want.
3. No way to note down the important gist of the paper as and when I read them.
The problem has become so severe that its demanding a solution. Here is my attempt.
2 STEP 1
Use biblatex and biber. Other ways of citing are getting old. Click me to learn how.
3 CENTRALIZED BIB FILE
It helps if you have a dropbox account or a personal website for this part of the solution. In case you
dont, just keep the bib le in a location (path) that doesnt change much and use it from there.
All you need is a url which can serve you your bib le. I maintain a central bib le as a part of my
website and have a url which serves me the le. Take a look at the link
https://nithishdivakar.github.io/resources/bibfile/
paperdb.bib
The \addbibresource command which usually helps you add a local le as a source for bib
entries also has a remote mode which accepts url.
\addbibresource[location=remote]{https://nithishdivakar.github.io
/resources/bibfile/paperdb.bib}
Now we have a centralized bib le and a way to import them into our document.
4 STORING GIST OF PAPERS
We have to store all the bibtex entries in a bib le anyway. So why not store the gist of papers in
them as well. But what about these gists showing up in our references ?
Turns out, every bibtex entry has a eld called abstract. The contents of this eld are almost always
not processed in every normal way of citation. So we can just go ahead and store the gist in this
eld. Ill illustrate with an example entry.

But when we want to read the abstract or we are in searching for what I read earlier mode, this
simple bib le wont help us.
To solve that we create a latex le which cites everything in this bib le and run compile.
\addbibresource[location=remote]{<url of paper>}
\begin{document}
\nocite{
*
}
:
:
\printbibliography
\end{document}
But we own get the abstracts. We only get the standard citations in our reference section. But, bib
latex has a style mode called reading. Just turn that on and compile.
\usepackage[backend=biber,style=reading]{biblatex}
We get back a pdf le which contains all papers in the bib le with their small gist printed in a nice
format. See the reference section for an example.
5 PUTTING IT ALL TOGETHER
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
%% paperDB.tex
\documentclass{article}
\usepackage[backend=biber,sorting=ydnt,style=reading]{biblatex}
\title{My Paper Database}
\addbibresource[location=remote]{<url of paper>}
\begin{document}
\maketitle
\nocite{
*
} %% cite everything
\printbibliography
\end{document}
